## About Me

As a professional who has been captivated by computing since my teenage years, I have built a career foundation rooted in networking, data center deployment, and infrastructure design. My transition into cybersecurity was sparked by a desire to become a more well-rounded professional, leading me from an initial SANS course in 2016 to my current pursuit of a Bachelor’s degree in Applied Cybersecurity at SANS Technology Institute. This rigorous academic journey has allowed me to earn eight GIAC certifications—including the GCIH, GCIA, and GICSP—providing the specialized knowledge and technical depth necessary to transition into a dedicated cybersecurity role.

My technical approach is deeply hands-on, emphasizing a proactive discovery of vulnerabilities and a methodical approach to defense. I am particularly active in the National Cyber League (NCL), where I participate in Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions to sharpen my forensic and technical skills. Beyond competitions, I maintain a home lab environment where I deploy and manage sophisticated security monitoring tools to analyze real-world threats and refine my automation capabilities.
